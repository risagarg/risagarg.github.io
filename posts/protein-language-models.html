What Does it Mean to Design Biology from Scratch? A Deep Dive into How Protein Language Models Work (Part I)

April 5, 2025

Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing protein design through "generative biology." Let me explain how this works.

Core Concept

Proteins function like sentences, where amino acids are the words. The sequence determines structure and function, much like word order determines meaning. This "grammar of proteins" can now be learned using artificial intelligence models similar to those powering ChatGPT.

Autoregressive Models

These models predict the next amino acid based on previous ones. They assign probability scores to sequences, enabling both generation of novel proteins and ranking of designed sequences by likelihood of functionality.

Masked Language Models

These fill in missing positions using bidirectional context. Interestingly, these models implicitly learn how the protein will fold despite training only on sequences, with attention patterns aligning to actual physical contact maps.

Technical Foundation

Both approaches rely on Transformer architecture, enabling simultaneous processing of entire sequences rather than sequential analysis. This breakthrough technology allows researchers at companies like Profluent to design completely novel biological tools, including new enzymes and CRISPR variants.
