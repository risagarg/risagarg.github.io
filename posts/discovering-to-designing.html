From Discovering Proteins to Designing Biology

April 10, 2025

Protein language models (PLMs) are transforming biological research from discovery to design. PLMs enable scientists to generate entirely new proteins rather than searching nature's libraries, marking a fundamental shift in biotech.

De Novo Protein Design

Profluent's OpenCRISPR-1 project generated a novel CRISPR-associated enzyme without natural templates. The protein functioned at high specificity for genome editing. Profluent's open-source strategy contrasts with typical patent approaches, betting that platform dominance matters more long-term than owning individual sequences.

Protein Evolution

LabGenius closes the loop between computational predictions and laboratory validation. Their platform learns dynamically from experimental results, enabling smarter protein optimization compared to traditional random mutagenesis approaches.

Drug Development

Absci incorporates manufacturability into models using paired sequence-expression datasets. This addresses a critical challenge: over 50% of antibody candidates fail preclinical stages due to instability or poor expression.

Future Directions

  • Condition-specific protein design for particular environments
  • Dynamic proteins that adapt to cellular signals
  • Industrial biocatalysis for carbon capture and clean energy

While exciting progress exists, real-world clinical deployment remains in early stages. The shift from biological discovery to design represents something transformative.
