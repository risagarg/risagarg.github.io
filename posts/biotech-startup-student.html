What It's Like to Build a Biotech Startup as a Student

April 23, 2025

As a college student majoring in biology and computer science, I want to discuss my experience building a biotech startup while juggling academic responsibilities. I challenge the perception that student founders are too inexperienced for the biotech field.

The Core Challenge

"Student founder ≠ side project." I'm conducting genuine scientific research alongside my studies, not pursuing a classroom assignment. My startup addresses a real scientific question with potential applications in therapy or materials design.

What VCs Should Understand

Rather than immediate funding, student biotech founders primarily need credibility and access—conversations with experienced scientists, datasets, and mentorship bridging technical depth with business strategy. The best thing a VC can do isn't to write a check, but rather ask what resources would help reach the next milestone.

Democratization of Biotech

Platforms like Benchling, Colab, and OpenFold, combined with protein language models and open-source biology, are lowering traditional barriers to entry in biotech innovation.

The Call to Action

Biotech doesn't need more gatekeeping. It needs more curiosity, more weird ideas, and more people bold enough to start without feeling "ready."
